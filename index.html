Derived Obsessed Graduate Students (DOGS) is an online junior seminar for graduate students working in Derived Categories. The seminar will take place fortnightly on Zoom (link coming soon!) on Tuesdays from 3pm to 4pm (London time) starting the 26th of September 2023. Note however that sometimes speakers may do a second talk in the subsequent week in support of their first more introductory talk.

The goal of this seminar, above all, is to be useful to its audience. Rather than a more research-oriented seminar, we hope this seminar to be a place where we can teach each other what we've learnt so far in our PhD. This is because the fastest and best way to learn is to have someone who has spent hours learning something explain it to you! If you'd like to speak or have any other questions, please contact Michela Barbieri (michela.barbieri.21@ucl.ac.uk) or Aporva Varshney (aporva.varshney.22@ucl.ac.uk).

Derived Categories of Nodal Varieties

Aporva Varshney, UCL
It can be daunting to leave the warm, cosy duvet of smooth varieties, no matter how much the minimal model program may tempt us. In this talk, we will take small steps by looking at nodal varieties. We will see how, at the level of the derived category, nodal singularities can sometimes be "absorbed" by producing a semiorthogonal decomposition into a "singular" component and a "smooth" component. Even more amazingly, we'll then see how for curves and threefolds admitting this kind of absorption, the singular component in the decomposition vanishes when smoothing the variety.
