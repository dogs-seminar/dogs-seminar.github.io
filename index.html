Derived Obsessed Graduate Students (DOGS) is an online junior seminar for graduate students working in Derived Categories. The seminar will take place fortnightly on zoom (link coming soon!) on Tuesdays from 3pm to 4pm GMT starting the 26th of September 2023. Note however that sometimes speakers may do a second talk in the subsequent week in support of their first more introductory talk.

The goal of this seminar, above all, is to be useful to its audience. Rather than a more research-oriented seminar, we hope this seminar to be a place where we can teach each other what we've learnt so far in our PhD. This is because the fastest and best way to learn is to have someone who has spent hours learning something explain it to you! If you'd like to speak or have any other questions, please contact Michela Barbieri (michela.barbieri.21@ucl.ac.uk).

How much chuck could a wood chuck chuck if a wood chuck could chuck wood?

Aporva Varshney, UCL
In this talk, we aim to answer the age old question of how much chuck a wood chuck could chuck if a wood chuck could chuck wood. In order to do this, we will first review the basic theory of wood chucking. If time permits, we will consider other chuckable goods.
